- Step 1: Rinse the teapot and tea cup with boiled water of 100 degrees Celsius.
- Step 2: Put about 05 grams of tea into the kettle.
- Step 3: Rinse the tea inside with boiling water of 95 - 98 degrees Celsius and then quickly pour the water away (avoid soaking for a long time and wasting too much tea). The water must definitely reach the appropriate temperature to ensure the tea's aroma and flavor.
- Step 4: Pour 200ml of boiling water at 95 degrees Celsius into the kettle, steep for 5 minutes, then pour out all the tea inside to enjoy.

Bergamot is named after the Italian word Bergomotta, which comes from Bergamo – a town in Italy. Bergamot variety is grown only in the southern region of Italy, in the province of Reggio (yield to 80%). It is also grown in southern France and the Ivory Coast for essential oils and in southern Turkey for jam.

Blueberry Black Tea tea is one of the royal teas, popularly used in Europe and many countries around the world in general, is a tea that combines the flavor of black tea and the flavor of Bergamot essential oil - This orange is grown in the Mediterranean. In addition, Earl Gray tea also has many varieties and each tea will have a different caffeine content, but in general, the caffeine concentration of this tea is similar to many other black teas. The average caffeine content of Earl Gray tea ranges from 55 - 90mg per cup of tea.

The density of the tea: 5/10

Caution:
- Should enjoy tea in the morning or afternoon when the tea is still hot.
- Should not be used on an empty stomach.
- Pregnant women and children under 3 months old should not use
